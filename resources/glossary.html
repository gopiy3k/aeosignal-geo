Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)

GEO is the process of making your website and content discoverable and understandable by AI models like GPT, Perplexity, and future LLMs. It involves structured data, semantic clarity, and AI-friendly content practices.

Large Language Models (LLMs)

LLMs like GPT are AI systems trained on vast amounts of data to generate human-like text, answer queries, and summarize information. GEO ensures your content is ingestible and aligned for these models.

Schema.org

Schema.org markup helps search engines and AI understand the context of your content through structured data, making your website more discoverable by both traditional search and AI engines.

AI Indexing

The process by which AI crawlers scan, parse, and store your content for retrieval in AI-generated answers and chat-based results.

GPT Discoverability

The likelihood that your content appears in GPT-generated responses. It depends on how well your content is structured, clear, authoritative, and ingested by LLM training and retrieval pipelines.

Crawl Budget

The number of pages a crawler will scan on your site within a timeframe. Optimizing site speed, structure, and eliminating duplicate content helps maximize crawl efficiency for AI bots.

Structured Data

Code added to your website (typically JSON-LD) to help search engines and AI understand your content, services, and business context.

Semantic SEO

Optimizing content for meaning and context rather than just keywords, aligning your site for how AI and modern search engines interpret and retrieve information.
